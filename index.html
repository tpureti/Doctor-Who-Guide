For those of you want to watch Classic Who without sitting through 600+ episodes, here are a selection of stories from each Doctor that I've personally deemed as "essential viewing."

These stories were chosen because they're either crucial to the lore (such as introducing Doctors, companions, and villains for the first time), have major cultural relevance, or are really well written, entertaining stories that showcase the best of Doctor Who.

Naturally, some of these picks are my opinion, but I tried to chose what would make a good breadth and variety of stories for first time viewers to experience.

Due to an old BBC policy of junking master tapes after the episodes stopping airing, a number of Doctor Who stories — all from the First and Second Doctor's eras — no longer exist and can only be experienced in audio, telesnapped, and animated form.

It's only because of tapes given to other countries to air on their own networks and the efforts of dedicated fans to find them that we have as many stories from the early days of Doctor Who as we do.

It wasn't until 1978 (that's well into the Fourth Doctor's era) that the BBC officially stopped doing this. However, since every story from the Third Doctor era onward is preserved, I only keep track of missing stories up to The War Games.

(You can read more about Doctor Who's missing episodes on wikipedia.)
